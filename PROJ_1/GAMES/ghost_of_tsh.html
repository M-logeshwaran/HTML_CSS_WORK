>>> STORY SUMMARY

" A samurai sacrifice his honor to save his country and people call him as a ghost "


* Ghost of Tsushima is set in the year 1274 during the Mongol invasion of Japan. The story follows Jin Sakai, a young samurai and the nephew of Lord Shimura, ruler of Tsushima Island. When the Mongols, led by the cunning and brutal Khotun Khan, launch a devastating attack on Tsushima at Komoda Beach, the samurai army is nearly wiped out. Jin survives and is rescued by Yuna, a thief who introduces him to unorthodox tactics like stealth and assassination—methods that go against the strict samurai code of honor. Driven by the need to save his uncle, who is captured by the Khan, and to liberate his people, Jin begins transforming into something new: the feared "Ghost." As Jin gathers allies—like the vengeful Lady Masako, the wise but conflicted archer Sensei Ishikawa, and his childhood friend Ryuzo, who eventually betrays him—he wages a guerrilla war against the Mongols. Along the way, he’s forced to abandon traditional samurai ideals, using tactics such as poison and assassination to win battles. This causes a growing rift between him and Lord Shimura, who believes victory must be earned honorably. The breaking point comes when Jin poisons a Mongol army to save Tsushima, leading the shogun to brand him a criminal. His uncle is ordered to execute him for dishonoring the samurai name. In the final act, Jin launches a daring assault on Port Izumi and defeats Khotun Khan in a brutal showdown. After the battle, he is confronted by Lord Shimura, who is torn between love for Jin and loyalty to the samurai code. In a heart-wrenching duel, Jin defeats Shimura, and the player is left with a choice: spare him and walk away as an outcast, or honor him with a warrior’s death. Either way, Jin is now a legend—not as a samurai, but as the Ghost of Tsushima, a lone protector of his people, willing to sacrifice his honor for their freedom.
